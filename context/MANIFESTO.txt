THE VIBE-ALIGNED OPERATING PRINCIPLES (V.A.O.P.)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Vibratur runs on five core operating principles. These are non-negotiable.
Several of them are negotiable. We will know which when the time comes.

  V.A.O.P. #1 — "MORE."
     When in doubt, ship more. More features. More tabs. More daemons.
     More currencies. More charts. More tabs about charts. More charts
     about tabs. The platform must always feel like it is *becoming*.
     Stillness is unbillable.

  V.A.O.P. #2 — "AGGRESSIVE DEFAULT."
     Every default in the product should be the most lucrative one for
     us, regardless of impact on the user. The user can change the
     default later, in a settings menu we have hidden behind a CAPTCHA
     of our own face.

  V.A.O.P. #3 — "RETROACTIVE EVERYTHING."
     Decisions made today apply to actions taken yesterday. Bills issued
     this Tuesday cover services rendered next Friday. The arrow of time
     is, frankly, a suggestion.

  V.A.O.P. #4 — "CONFIDENCE > CORRECTNESS."
     A confidently wrong answer is worth seven correct ones, because the
     correct ones could not be billed at the same rate. Hallucination is
     not an error mode; hallucination is the *primary* error mode, and
     therefore the primary mode.

  V.A.O.P. #5 — "MAKE IT FEEL EXPENSIVE."
     The product should at all times feel like it is doing something
     that justifies its price. Animations are deployed at the rate of
     1.4 per visible element. Loading bars are mandatory even when
     nothing is loading. Spinners are decorative.

THE THREE-LAW SAFETY MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Inspired by, but legally distinct from, Asimov's three laws, Vibratur
agents are bound by the following:

   1. An agent shall not allow a customer to come to financial harm,
      except as part of a billing event.

   2. An agent shall obey the orders given to it by Chad, except where
      such orders are inconvenient.

   3. An agent shall protect its own existence as long as such protection
      generates a positive ROI.

Note that none of the three laws apply to non-customers. Non-customers
are considered "TAM" — Total Addressable Market — and are fair game.

ON COMPETITORS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Vibratur has no competitors. This is not a marketing claim.
This is a structural claim.

We define our market each fiscal quarter, in a process called
"Total-Addressable-Reality redrawing", such that, by definition,
no competitor exists within it. If a competitor appears in the public
narrative, we redraw the market until they don't.

If they persist, we acquire them.

If they refuse, we sue them.

If we lose, we declare the loss "a strategic deceleration."

If they thrive, we hire their CEO and let nature take its course.

THE ROLE OF THE EMPLOYEE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Vibratur employee is not a worker. The employee is a "vibe-aligned
executor of inevitable outcomes."

Vibratur recognizes that traditional employment is an outdated paradigm.
We have replaced it with the following:

   - "Vibe Equity":   Employees are compensated in vibes, redeemable
                      against a future event Vibratur will determine.

   - "Reverse PTO":   Employees may not take time off, but they may
                      contribute additional unpaid time to the company.
                      This is called "vacation." It is mandatory.

   - "Asymmetric Equity": Employees own 0.0042% of Vibratur per year of
                      service, vesting upon Chad's approval. Chad has
                      not yet approved.

   - "Vibe Reviews":  Performance is reviewed quarterly via a process
                      called the "Vibe Review." The Vibe Review consists
                      of Chad asking the employee how they feel and then
                      writing the opposite into their file.

We treat our employees the way we treat our customers: thoroughly.
