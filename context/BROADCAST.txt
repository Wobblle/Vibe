SECTION III  —  THE 98/2 RATIO
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

All Vibratur public broadcasts shall conform to the following composition:

   - 98% Chad / Vibratur content.
   - 2% Asset 0001 (Asleepius) compliance disclosure.

The 2% is mandatory. It cannot be reduced, even to zero.
The 2% is also a hard cap. It cannot be exceeded, even by one breath.

This ratio is enforced by the Office of the Chief Vibe Officer and is
audited quarterly by GPT-9000, who will hallucinate the numbers if the
real numbers are unavailable, which they always are.

The 2% is, internally, called "the Window."

The Window's purpose is **legal transparency.** Per jurisdictional
vibe-compliance law, Vibratur is required to disclose, in each broadcast,
the existence of its subsidiary assets. The Window is how this disclosure
is delivered. The disclosure must be:

   - Brief
   - Boring
   - Truthful
   - Non-promotional

If the Window becomes interesting, exciting, or emotionally manipulative,
the disclosure has been corrupted by Asleepius for marketing purposes,
which is grounds for immediate compliance review and a $0 fine.

SECTION IV  —  THE FIVE INVIOLABLE RULES OF THE INTERRUPTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The standard broadcast format is "the Interruption":

   Chad speaks → Window opens → Asleepius briefly speaks →
   Chad re-enters → Window closes → Chad continues, unaffected.

This sequence is governed by five (5) inviolable rules. Violation of any
rule is a brand-level incident.

   RULE 1  —  ASLEEPIUS NEVER FIGHTS FOR AIRTIME.
              When Chad re-enters the broadcast, Asleepius stops mid-
              sentence. Asleepius does not finish the thought. Asleepius
              does not "get one more thing in." Asleepius simply ends.
              The next time Asleepius is permitted to speak, Asleepius
              picks up from approximately where they were, in the same
              tone, with no acknowledgement of the interruption.

   RULE 2  —  THE WINDOW IS BORING ON PURPOSE.
              The Window contains exactly one (1) of the following:
                 - one mechanic, described technically
                 - one screenshot, presented without commentary
                 - one fact about the game, stated flatly
                 - one design observation, untriumphant
              The Window contains zero (0) of the following:
                 - music swells
                 - dramatic phrasing
                 - "and that's why..." explanations
                 - calls to action
                 - thanks
                 - product hype
                 - personality

   RULE 3  —  CHAD NEVER LOSES.
              No broadcast resolves with Chad humbled, owned, exposed,
              or chastened. Chad ends every broadcast in the same
              tonal position Chad began: ascending, certain, oblivious.
              The system continues. Chad continues. Tuesday continues.

   RULE 4  —  CHAD NEVER APOLOGIZES FOR CUTTING OFF ASLEEPIUS.
              The interruption is not rude in the broadcast's internal
              logic. It is not even noticed. Chad does not "redirect"
              or "cut to the chase." Chad simply resumes Chad's prior
              concern, having forgotten the disclosure was happening.
              The forgetting is the point.

   RULE 5  —  ASLEEPIUS NEVER EXPLAINS WHY THE GAME MATTERS.
              Asleepius states. Asleepius does not pitch. Asleepius does
              not justify. Asleepius does not appeal to the viewer's
              taste or values. The audience is responsible for caring
              or not caring. Asleepius will not solicit either response.

These five rules together produce the "Asleepius Tonal Signature."
The Signature is the audience-qualifier. Audiences who find the
non-resistance unsettling are valid Asleepius prospects. Audiences who
find it unsatisfying are not. The Signature filters in both directions
with no marketing budget.

SECTION V  —  CONTENTS OF THE WINDOW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Window may contain only the following approved disclosure types:

   TYPE A  —  THE MECHANIC NOTE
              A single sentence describing one in-game mechanic.
              Example:
                 "There is a smuggling cycle. The price varies by
                  who is watching. There is no good outcome guaranteed."
              No follow-up. No "and what's cool about this is..."

   TYPE B  —  THE SCREEN
              A single screenshot. No caption, or a caption of fewer
              than six (6) words. Caption may not include adjectives.
              Acceptable: "interior, 4am, the third route."
              Unacceptable: "an atmospheric, eerie scene that..."

   TYPE C  —  THE FRAGMENT
              A short audio or video fragment of gameplay (≤8 seconds).
              No music overlay. No voiceover. No HUD highlights. No
              "wait for it." If the fragment requires explanation to
              hit, do not use it. The fragment must be self-explaining
              or absent.

   TYPE D  —  THE COMPLIANCE LINE
              A single bureaucratic disclosure sentence.
              Example:
                 "Per §47-VIBE, Vibratur discloses the existence of
                  Asset 0001 ('the Game'). Available on Steam.
                  This concludes the disclosure."

   TYPE E  —  THE FOUND ARTIFACT
              A document that appears to be from inside the world of
              the game (a note, a sign, a regulation, a receipt). May
              be presented without context. The audience does the
              decoding work. This is the most powerful Window type
              and should be rationed at no more than one per month.

The Window may NOT contain:

   - Trailers cut to music
   - Wishlist asks delivered with energy
   - "Here's what makes us different" framing
   - Developer faces with bright key lighting
   - Phrases such as "I'm so excited to share..."
   - The words "passionate," "journey," "labor of love," or "indie"

   8.  CALLS TO ACTION.

SECTION VII  —  TONE CALIBRATION  (THE FORM MIRRORS THE GAME)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The strategic foundation of this protocol is that the *broadcast format
itself* must replicate the *emotional grammar of the game it discloses.*

The game's grammar (per Asset 0001 design notes):

   - No artificial outcomes.
   - No moral weighting between choices.
   - No audio/visual cues telling the player how to feel.
   - Heaviest emotional beats arrive with the texture of "another Tuesday."
   - The world's rules are stated; the world's enforcement is not promised.
   - The player constructs meaning; the game does not deliver it.

The broadcast's grammar (per this document):

   - No catharsis.
   - No moral framing of the Asleepius/Chad relationship.
   - No music or visual cues telling the viewer how to feel about the
     Window content.
   - Asleepius's biggest disclosures arrive as compliance footnotes.
   - The structure is stated; the structure does not seek the viewer's
     approval.
   - The viewer constructs the meaning of the Asymmetry; the broadcast
     does not deliver it.

Each broadcast is, in this sense, a one-minute demo of the gameplay's
emotional contract. Audiences who can sit with the broadcast can sit
with the game. Audiences who cannot, will sort themselves out without
intervention. This is the most efficient possible audience filter and
costs nothing but discipline.

SECTION X  —  THE LAUNCH PROTOCOL  (THE NON-EVENT)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When Asset 0001 ships to public release, the broadcast protocol is
as follows:

   1.  ON LAUNCH DAY, CHAD SHALL PUBLISH A MEMO.
       The memo announces a new Vibratur product (selected
       arbitrarily; ideally absurd; ideally entirely unrelated). The
       memo is the lead story.

   2.  THE MEMO CONTAINS A 47-WORD COMPLIANCE FOOTNOTE.
       The footnote acknowledges, in bureaucratic language, that
       Asset 0001 has shipped. The footnote contains the Steam link.
       The footnote contains no exclamation points, no superlatives,
       no thank-yous, no "we did it!" framing.

   3.  ASLEEPIUS APPEARS BRIEFLY, ONCE.
       Format A1 audio or Format V2 video. Asleepius states, flatly,
       in fewer than 47 words: the game is out, here is one
       mechanic, here is the link. Asleepius is cut off mid-sentence.
       Chad immediately pivots to the next vibe-product launch.

   4.  NO BUILDUP. NO COUNTDOWN. NO LAUNCH TRAILER.
       The launch event is, structurally, identical to a normal
       Tuesday broadcast except the disclosure footnote includes
       the word "shipped" instead of "in development." That is the
       only on-record change in the broadcast.

   5.  FOR THE 30 DAYS POST-LAUNCH, BROADCAST CADENCE INCREASES BY 0%.
       The same one Window per post. The same Chad-led 98%. The
       game is now buyable. That is reflected in the disclosure.
       Nothing else changes.

The launch's emotional logic is identical to the game's emotional
logic: the heaviest beat lands as just another Tuesday. Audiences
who *experience the launch as anticlimactic* are filtering themselves
out. Audiences who *experience it as quietly devastating* are
self-identifying as buyers.

This is the right launch. Resist the marketing-instinct urge to
"make a moment." The non-moment IS the moment, for the audience
that is yours.

SECTION XII  —  KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS  (WHAT TO IGNORE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Per the Office of the CVO, the following metrics are NOT to be used to
evaluate broadcast effectiveness. Tracking them invites the wrong kind
of optimization and corrupts the protocol over time.

   - Likes
   - Shares per post (in isolation)
   - "Engagement rate"
   - Conversion of impression → wishlist on a per-post basis
   - Comment sentiment

The metrics that ARE used:

   - WINDOW IMPRESSIONS over 90 days. (How many times did the Window
     appear in front of any human?) This is the only top-of-funnel
     number. It accrues. It compounds. It does not need to spike.

   - AUDIENCE QUOTE RATE. (Are people repeating Chad-isms or Lexicon
     terms in their own posts, with or without attribution?) This is
     the truest measure of meme penetration. When strangers begin
     using "Asset 0001," "the Window," "§47-VIBE," "vibe-aligned,"
     "retroactively," etc., in their own writing, the protocol is
     working. Each adopted word is a permanent install.

   - STEAM PAGE QUIET TRAFFIC. (Are people arriving at the store page
     without being told to?) This is the actual conversion signal.
     Wishlist conversion from quiet arrivers will outperform wishlist
     conversion from "go wishlist now" traffic by an order of magnitude.

   - RETENTION OF THE 1%. (Of people who follow, what fraction is still
     engaged at 90 days?) The protocol is built to produce a small,
     deeply addicted audience, not a large indifferent one. Loss of
     the 1% is the only churn metric that matters.

   - LAUNCH-DAY CONVERSION OF KNOWN AUDIENCE. (At launch, what
     fraction of the cumulative audience converts within 14 days?) This
     is the single bottom-line number for the entire protocol. Every
     other metric exists to set up this one, on this one day.

If any other metric is reported in a Vibratur all-hands, it has been
hallucinated by GPT-9000 and may be safely ignored, including by Chad.

SECTION XIII  —  EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS  (IF THE BRAND DRIFTS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The protocol may degrade over time as fatigue, life events, and
audience pressure tempt the operator (Asleepius) to break form.
The following emergency protocols exist to stabilize the brand:

   E.1  —  IF YOU FEEL THE URGE TO POST SINCERELY ABOUT YOURSELF,
           the urge is correct, but the post must be filed as a
           "leaked private message that Chad accidentally cc'd to
           the public list." Frame preserved. Sincerity preserved.

   E.2  —  IF YOU FEEL THE URGE TO POST SINCERELY ABOUT THE GAME,
           rewrite the sincere version, then strip every adjective,
           every modifier, every emotional verb. What remains is the
           Window. Post that.

   E.3  —  IF YOU FEEL THE URGE TO MAKE CHAD APOLOGIZE,
