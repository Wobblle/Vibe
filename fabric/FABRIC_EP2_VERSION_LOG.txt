WHAT THIS IS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A version log of a single English-language word — "freedom" — formatted
as if produced by a package manager. The compressed version of this
artifact is read aloud in episode 2.0 in approximately 30 seconds.
This is the full version. Read it slowly. The shape of the drift is
the artifact, not the specific entries.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DISCLAIMER  ·  READ FIRST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The version numbers, dates, and binding texts in this log are
ILLUSTRATIVE, NOT historically authoritative. They are designed to make
the SHAPE of semantic drift legible at a glance. They are not, and do
not claim to be, a citable history of the word "freedom."

For real conceptual history of "freedom" in the Anglo-American tradition:

   - Eric Foner. The Story of American Freedom. W. W. Norton, 1998.
   - Quentin Skinner. Liberty Before Liberalism. Cambridge, 1998.

For the underlying framework of conceptual history (Begriffsgeschichte):

   - Reinhart Koselleck. Futures Past: On the Semantics of
     Historical Time. MIT Press translation, 1985.

The shape is real. The specific entries are gestural.

LOG
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


$ npm view freedom

freedom@1.0.0   c. 1789      "absence of arbitrary state coercion;
                              individual standing under enumerated law"
                              BREAKING (from feudal binding):
                              freedom now grounded in legal individual
                              status rather than birthright privilege.

freedom@1.4.0   c. 1791      "+ enumerated protections against state
                              power: speech, assembly, press, religion"

freedom@1.8.0   c. 1830s     "+ extended to non-property-holding
                              white men in most Anglo polities"

freedom@2.0.0   c. 1865      "+ absence of chattel ownership of
                              persons (Reconstruction-era amendments)"
                              BREAKING: legal-status semantics
                              earlier versions are now incomplete.

freedom@2.1.4   c. 1875      DEPRECATED IN PRACTICE:
                              southern U.S. operational binding rolled
                              back to ~1.8 series via local enforcement
                              regimes (Jim Crow). Spec disagrees with
                              implementation. No patch published.

freedom@2.7.0   c. 1920      "+ extended to women via suffrage in most
                              Anglo polities"

freedom@2.9.0   c. 1933-39   "+ collective economic protections;
                              freedom from want included as a positive
                              freedom (FDR's four freedoms framing)"
                              BREAKING: introduces positive freedoms
                              as legitimate alongside negative ones.

freedom@3.0.0   c. 1948      "+ universal individual baseline
                              (Universal Declaration of Human Rights)"
                              BREAKING: international consensus binding;
                              freedom now claimed as a universal
                              property of human persons.

freedom@3.5.0   c. 1964-65   "+ legal protections against private
                              discrimination (Civil Rights Act, Voting
                              Rights Act)"

freedom@3.8.0   c. 1970s     "+ reproductive autonomy (in some
                              jurisdictions); + bodily integrity as
                              defended freedom"

freedom@4.0.0   c. 1980      "primarily market-participation freedom;
                              economic regulation reframed as freedom
                              violation"
                              BREAKING: rebinds 'freedom' against the
                              positive-freedoms binding from @2.9 and
                              @3.0. Earlier collective protections are
                              quietly deprecated.

freedom@4.5.0   c. 1990s     DEPRECATED: collective economic
                              freedoms.
                              the @2.9.0 and parts of @3.0.0 bindings
                              for freedom-from-want and freedom-from-
                              fear are operationally retired without
                              formal removal from spec.

freedom@5.0.0   c. 2001      "context-dependent; security override
                              clause"
                              BREAKING: introduces compatibility with
                              mass surveillance, indefinite detention,
                              and pre-trial restriction. Conflicts with
                              @1.0.0 and @1.4.0 spec text are resolved
                              in favor of @5.0.0 by precedent.

freedom@5.4.0   c. 2010s     "+ freedom of platform speech contested;
                              private-corporate gatekeeping reframed
                              as private-property right"
                              the original @1.4.0 speech protection is
                              now scoped to state-only actors; private-
                              actor capacity to silence speech is
                              compatible with current binding.

freedom@5.7.0   c. 2015-19   FRAGMENTED: significant population
                              segments running incompatible bindings.
                              users self-sort by which @-version they
                              are loyal to. interoperability between
                              segments approaches zero.

freedom@6.0.0   c. 2020      "freedom-to-not-be-inconvenienced override
                              of collective public-health logic"
                              BREAKING: individual override of
                              statistical population-level reasoning;
                              negative freedoms reasserted against
                              positive-freedom bindings retained from
                              @2.9 / @3.0.

freedom@6.1.3   c. 2024+     CURRENT.
                              widely contested between fragmented
                              user-bases. spec text not authoritatively
                              maintained. binding decided by whoever
                              speaks loudest at the moment of the
                              specific claim. interoperability with
                              prior versions is per-conversation.

WHAT TO DO WITH THIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When you next find yourself in a political argument that seems to be
about facts but somehow refuses to resolve, ask: what version of the
word "freedom" is each speaker running? The same question applies to
"community," "friend," "privacy," "success," "attention," "memory,"
"consent," "mental health," "self-care," "wellness," "safety," and
"truth."

Most political conversations in current English-language discourse
are version-mismatch conversations. The participants assume they are
arguing about the world. They are arguing about the operational
binding of shared symbols whose specs were never publicly maintained.

You will not win these conversations. You may, however, be able to
recognize what kind of conversation they are.
