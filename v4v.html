sky scaffold

a video game by asleepius games · solo-developed
wishlist now price set at release · no subscription, ever · no dlc, ever
what it is

sky scaffold is a 2d pixel-art game. you build an outpost on the edge of an inhabited region of space, route logistics, dispatch ships, defend against invasions. it's a hybrid of industrial management, card-based auto-battle combat, and dispatch / expedition logistics. paced slow.

there's a story underneath. it isn't delivered through dialogue trees or quest logs. it's delivered through documents that npcs in the world print, carry, lose, and occasionally die with. there are factions. the factions have philosophies. the philosophies don't agree. you piece things together by paying attention. some of what you piece together is hostile, in the sense that the world isn't on your side.

the numbers go up. the factory works. and if you read the right documents in the right order, you start to notice the factory is being used for something its operators haven't told you about.

underneath the game there's a separate system being built called the phantom narrative engine. pre-generated scripts, syntax-driven text generation, and updates produced on op's local hardware, then synced to the game over time and shaped by what other players are doing. nothing leaves op's studio for any third party. no subscription is attached. the meta-gameplay it powers is included in the price of the game forever.

that part is in development. that part is the part op is most excited about. the flavor of narrative tone it aims at is roughly what you've been reading across vibratur — the parser, the hallucinated docs, the agent voices. that's the register. that's what's being built toward.
